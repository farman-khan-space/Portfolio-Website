Mission & Tone of Voice

The brand's mission is to transform complex data into actionable insights and compelling narratives. We aim to make data "speak" in a way that is clear, accessible, and impactful for any audience.

Tone of Voice

  • Professional & Insightful: Content should be well-researched, accurate, and demonstrate expertise.
  • Clear & Accessible: Avoid jargon. Explain complex topics simply. The goal is to empower, not confuse.
  • Story-driven: Frame data analysis as a narrative. Every dataset has a story to tell.

Logo

The primary logo consists of a stylized lightbulb icon with a bar chart inside, representing an "insight" or "idea" derived from data. It is paired with the logotype "datamakingsense.space".

Usage

  • The full logo should be used whenever possible, especially in headers and official documents.
  • The standalone lightbulb icon can be used as a favicon or social media profile picture.
  • Ensure sufficient clear space around the logo. Do not stretch or alter its proportions.

Typography

Heading Font: Poppins (Bold)

Market Trend Analysis

Used for all main headings (h1, h2, h3) to provide a bold, modern, and professional feel. It should always be used in a heavy weight (700).

Body Font: Inter

This is the body text, used for paragraphs and other general content. Inter is a clean, highly readable sans-serif font that works beautifully for longer-form text, ensuring a comfortable reading experience for users on any device.

UI Components

Reusable components form the building blocks of the website, ensuring a consistent user experience.

Buttons

Buttons are used for primary calls to action. They should be distinct and provide clear feedback on hover and active states.

Primary Button Secondary Button

Cards

Cards are used to display summarized content for projects and blog posts. They use a `card-glass` and `bg-noise` style for a subtle texture. On hover, the card flips to reveal more details.

The card container has a minimum height of 450px to ensure consistency across the grid.

Imagery & Icons

Card Images

Images for project and blog cards are crucial for visual appeal. To maintain consistency and avoid distortion in the flip-card component, all card images should adhere to a specific aspect ratio.

  • Aspect Ratio: 3:4 (Portrait)
  • Recommended Resolution: 1200px wide by 1600px tall.
  • Content: The most important visual elements should be centered to ensure they remain visible on all screen sizes.

Icons

The website uses the Bootstrap Icons library for functional icons like the mobile menu toggle. Icons should be used sparingly and consistently.

Animation & Interactivity

Subtle animations are used to enhance the user experience without being distracting. The primary library used is AOS (Animate on Scroll).

  • Page Load: Most content sections use a subtle fade-up or fade effect to reveal themselves as the user scrolls.
  • Card Hover: Project and blog cards use a CSS-only 3D flip animation to reveal details on the back.
  • Smooth Scroll: All on-page anchor links (e.g., in a table of contents) scroll smoothly to their destination. This is enabled with scroll-behavior: smooth;.
